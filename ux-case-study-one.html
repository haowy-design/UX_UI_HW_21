Overview

Given Australians are all required to handle their own tax and super, and the ATO has been pushing people to visit their online website for any useful information and tools. However, using the ATO website can be an intimidating experience, especially for those who are less tech-savvy or don’t understand much about their finances. Therefore, there was a need to overhaul the website, to make it modern, user-friendly, and efficient for users to locate the relevant information and functions they require.

I was the main UX/UI designer for this redesign of the ATO website in early 2023, and was heavily involved with two others in conducting UX research prior to conceptualizing a solution.

The Problem

Most first-time users and even familiar users struggle to navigate the website when trying to reach an intended page. There is an overwhelming amount of information that is not well structured making it difficult for users to filter and locate what they are looking for. Many users eventually give up on navigating the website and use the search function or completely dismiss the website and go through in person or over the phone methods.

Goals

  • User: Access information and tools in regards to taxation.

  • Business: Increase accuracy of user input and decrease the number of calls regarding website issues.

  • Product: User-friendly hub for people to access information and tools for their financial needs.


Simplifying and making the navigation more intuitive and flexible so users can easily find the page they are looking for.

Research

Usability tests were carried out on the ATO website to investigate the underlying issues and identify the affected user groups. The data revealed that while most users had a clear understanding of the information or functionality they sought, they encountered challenges in locating the content due to inadequate navigation and confusing terminology. Additionally, some users managed to find the desired page but inadvertently overlooked the necessary buttons required to initiate certain website functions. This oversight was primarily attributed to the overwhelming volume of information presented and the way it was structured on the site.

How it Works

Minimise Clutter & Reconstruction

Reducing the amount of information users see on the screen makes it more welcoming and easier to read the information. In addition to laying out the content logically helps users scan the page more quickly as well.

Focusing on Search Function

Even after simplification, the navigation bar may still be a little too many steps for some users. Thus, we have placed the search bar as a prominent feature to cater to those who prefer that type of navigation method. Now users have both options readily available to them.

Label information and highlight CTA buttons

All the information was restructured in a logical manner, so users are able to easily find & read the information most relevant to the them. Any items like buttons and tools were repositioned and outlined to ensure users don’t miss it as they browse through the information.

Results

Based on feedback from user testing, we received positive reception about the website redesign. We found that different users were using methods of navigation that they were most comfortable with; 50:50, either the search bar or the navigation bar. The time it took for users to reach their intended page was also reduced substantially, which highlights that the solution has not only become user-friendly but efficient as well.
